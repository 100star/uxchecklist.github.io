Research

See how others solve similar problem, and try to not reinvent the wheel. Read more

Do you have all the useful data you need? Try to have a look at funnels, clicks, page views, performances... Read more

Always speak with Customer Care team! Not having one? Check your old surveys or videos, what your customer says? What they actually do? Read more

Plan

Hope you got your personas done yet. If not DO IT NOW. Ok, now use them to write down user stories and scenarios. Read more

Create your user's flow based on the scenarios you created, you can use it later to review the journey and create wireframes on top of each step. Read more

Define red routes for your product and you’ll be able to identify, prioritise and eliminate any usability obstacles on the key user journeys. Read more

Explore

Find a war room, fill it with markers and drinks, get together: sketch, discuss, vote, disrupt, have fun! Read more

Add some details and structure to your ideas, reuse patterns and create pages on top of your user flows so you'll not leave anything back. Read more

You can start creating paper prototypes and continuosly iterate to get more functional ones. Use sketches, HTML pages or static images, just get some people and test it. Read more

Communicate

Understand your users, your data structure and your channels. How can you organise your navigation and content in a clear and consistent way? Read more

Follow your brand personality, keep in mind users' culture and language, the context of your product and make sure they understand you. Read more

You don’t need to add extra functionality or to duplicate any content. The key is simply to assess the requirements of those with different skills and limited devices. Read more

Create

Reuse what you already got, follow your style guidelines, not have one? Create your guidelines. Think small, then create pages. Read more

So you've have a swipe slider? Tell me more about pinch, drag, zoom, rotate, shake, 6" smartphones, left handed people, mouseover, kinect, motion detection... Read more

Can I see it on my mobile? Oh wait, what about my smart-watch which work as a remote for my 50" TV. Bonus: remember cross device experience. Read more

Give feedback

If your users have to wait ages for load a video of your cat, at least show them a loader, if take longer why don't you try something more enterteining? Read more

Be clear and specific on what and where user's error is. I mean, your error, because if it's your fault you should say it. Read more

Give immediate and clear feedback of successful user's actions. Do not always wait for server response, trust your server once in a while! Read more

Finalise

It's time to let your design shine, make it in the right way, don't stop at the first solution, always ask "is this the best you can do?" Read more

Use of icons and images is strongly influenced by context, culture and layout that you use. Like icons, test your images, small changes can bring huge improvements. Read more

Use colors and font sizes properly and wisely, follow your guidelines and keep it simple. The best visual hierarchies lead users to take the action confidently. Read more

Delight

Every word is important, and a bit of personality will help your brand. Read more

Trigger, rules, feedback, loop. Details make the product. ...Ever heard about easter eggs? Read more

Motion in the world of material design is not only beautiful, it builds meaning about the spatial relationships, functionality, and intention of the system. Read more

Analyse

What you want to achieve? What are your goals? Write down how you define success and insuccess and check if you have everything you need to collect the data. Read more

Plan your AB test ahead and if you can plan a short roadmap of improvements. Your goal is not just improve KPIs, but learn something. Read more

UX lab, survey, sessions recording... test, observe and fix, test, observer and fix... Read more
